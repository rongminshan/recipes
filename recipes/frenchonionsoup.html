French Onion Soup

Description

This delicious soup is perfect for any occasion. Serves 6 people.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 6 large yellow onions, diced
  • 3 tbs dry sherry
  • 1 tsp sherry vinegar, or to taste
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme
  • salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 6 slices French Bread
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup shredded extra-sharp Cheddar cheese
  • 1 cup shredded Montery Jack Cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F.
  2. Melt 1/4 cup butter in a large, oven safe skillet on medium heat. Stir onions until they are all coated. Transfer skilled to preheated onion and cook, stirring occasionally until tender and well browned, or about 1 hour.
  3. Transfer skillet to stovetop; cook and stir onions over medium heat until they start to brown and stick to the bottom, about 5 minutes. Pour sherry and vinegar in the pan, and bring to a boil while scraping browned bits of onions with a spoon.
  4. Place carmelized onions in a large soup pot. Pour chicken and beef broth, and thyme. Bring to a boil, skimming off foam or fat that appear on the top. Reduce heat to low and simmer for about an hour. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Preheat oven's broiler and set the oven rack 6 inches from the heat source.
  6. Brush French bread with generous amount of melted butter. Place on baking sheet and broil until crip and golden, or about 5 minutes.
  7. Ladle soup into heat-proof bowls, top each bowl with the toasted bread, and sprinkle with 2 to 3 tablespoons of Chedder and Monterey Jack cheese.
  8. Broil bowls under the broiler until cheese is golden and bubbly, ot 5 to 6 minutes.
